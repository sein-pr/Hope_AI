Understanding Uranium

Uranium is a dense, radioactive metal naturally found in the Earth's crust. Discovered in 1789, it powers nuclear energy with minimal carbon emissions.

Physical & Chemical Properties

A silvery-grey metal with atomic number 92, uranium’s isotope U-235 is fissile, enabling nuclear chain reactions for vast energy output.

Why Uranium Matters

Uranium’s fission in reactors generates heat to produce steam, driving turbines for electricity without CO₂ emissions, unlike fossil fuels.

Did you know? A fingertip-sized uranium pellet can generate energy equivalent to 1 ton of coal or 3 barrels of oil!

Global Impact

Namibia’s Uranium Wealth

Namibia ranks among the world’s top uranium producers, with rich deposits in the Erongo region mined by companies like Rossing and Husab.

Safety and Handling

Natural uranium emits low radiation. Namibia follows strict IAEA and national protocols for safe mining, transport, and storage.

Deep Dive

Uranium Geology

Explore the geological formations hosting uranium deposits and how they’re identified.

Found in porous sedimentary rocks, sandstone deposits form in river or delta environments, a key source in Namibia.

Uranium concentrates in fractures or veins within igneous rocks like granite, common in certain Namibian regions.

Process

Uranium Mining Process

Open-Pit Mining

Extracts shallow uranium deposits through surface excavation, used in many Namibian mines.

In-Situ Leaching

Dissolves uranium underground using solutions, reducing surface disruption.

Environmental Safeguards

Advanced technologies and strict protocols minimize ecological impact and ensure site rehabilitation.
